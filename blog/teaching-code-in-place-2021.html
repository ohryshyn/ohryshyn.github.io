Teaching Code in Place 2021

Last year, almost at the beginning of the COVID-19 pandemic, I attended a five-week Python programming course. Organized by Stanford University, it was available to everyone around the world. This course has become my starting journey into coding, which turned into a life-changing experience. After Code in Place, I knew not only how to write scripts but how to approach the problem, apply the logic and collaborate with international learners and teachers.

Applying as a Section Leader

A year has passed, and Code in Place was organized once again. The moment I received an email from Stanford University inviting me to apply as a Section Leader for Code in Place 2021, I knew that it was an opportunity not to be missed. Last year Code in Place gave me an astounding head start in Python, and I was thrilled to experience the program from the teacher’s perspective this time. My motivation coming into the course was simple - I wanted to pay back to the community that had nourished my coding skills at the beginning of the pandemic. And besides that, mentorship simply makes me happy.

As a part of the application, I had to prepare a pilot video of me teaching and explaining a Python problem to an imaginary class! It was not my first time recording a video, but the thrill of sharing it with the Stanford committee stumbled me.

  • How do I impress them?
  • What should I say to showcase my coding experience?
  • What teaching style should I use?

Impostor syndrome was on the radar. Not everything went smoothly at first. After a couple of promising attempts mumbling and trying to cover all possible material, I have decided to be myself and just roll over with the submission. I turned on the camera and started talking about variables and functions with passion without any hesitation… In the end, it turned out pretty well and lively, so I have submitted my application. To my surprise, the reply did not leave itself waiting for long. I got officially accepted as a Section Leader to teach Code in Place 2021 at Stanford University.

Leaders' Training

Right off the bat, I dived into the world of Code in Place. At the kick-off meeting, all Section Leaders got introduced to the background and principles that Code in Place was built on. Furthermore, we got a chance to meet face to face with all the amazing people who made this global initiative possible. As a part of the onboarding, we had two mentoring sessions that provided essential training on topics such as group learning, diversity, equity and inclusion. As my first teaching section was nigh, I felt well-equipped with the knowledge pouring the confidence into me. I got the answers to the worrisome questions:

  • How to capture students' attention?
  • How to create an uplifting learning environment in the online setting?
  • How to ensure that all students have a chance to have their voice heard?

For me, it became clear that teaching was about supporting student learning.

Teaching and Improving

Hosted on the OhYay platform, it was time to start teaching. To be honest, at first, I was expecting that I would do a lot of lecturing. However, to my surprise, I was learning more than I was teaching. The conversations that I had with students during our weekly sections reminded me of the importance of the fundamental programming concepts. Our sections made me go back to a year ago when I was still embracing the Python grounds and trying to figure out why the console is infinitely printing the output.

Just over five weeks, our class has leaped from zero Python experience to building a personal project with confidence! We have covered functions, control flow, decomposition and that was just the beginning! We have worked with images and learnt how to read and manipulate files. Every week, students had an opportunity to share feedback about the section and rate it! This feature has immensely helped me to continuously improve my teaching skills by adapting to students' needs and learning pace.

Moreover, to further hone the learning experience, Code in Place has introduced a weekly section transcript powered by AI. Besides allowing the review of the conversations, it showed the percentages of student interactions throughout the section. On top of that, it prompted me to reflect on the conversations while providing some tips for fostering communication. Even though the measure by AI was sometimes off, it was a nice tool to keep me aware of the section flow.

Closing words

Code in Place 2021 has given me an outstanding opportunity to jump straight into the world of teaching. I have embraced the joy of sharing knowledge and felt genuine happiness seeing my students progress.

In the past, I might have had some doubts about whether I wanted to pursue teaching education, but now I am determined that someday I would love to become an educator. It was the first time in my life that I was teaching someone in an online setting, and it will not be my last time supporting an initiative like this.
